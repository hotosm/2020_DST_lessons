 CALL TO ACTION 

Maps are critical in the hours, days, and weeks after a disaster to help the right people and the right supplies get to the right place, at the right time, increasing the potential to save lives. In 2020, HOT has led the coordination of 5 mapping responses to disastrous events -also known as Activations- and played a supporting role in 4 other responses occurring around the globe, all amid a global pandemic crisis. Would you like to contribute to HOT’s humanitarian mission?

Besides mapping at the Tasking Manager, you can spread the word directly with friends and family, and by tagging @HOTOSM in your Social Media. Additionally, if you are an experienced OpenStreetMap contributor, you could become a Validator and help us ensure high-quality data is delivered to responders and aid agencies, sign up in our validator application page Last, if you’re over-the-top dedicated like we are and want to be a part of the Disaster Services Team (DST), you can start by attending meetings of the Activation Working Group. Ask how in our #disaster-mapping Slack channel.

 ASIA-PACIFIC TYPHOONS 


The first Tropical Storm of the North Indian Ocean Cyclone Season became Super Cyclonic Storm Amphan. The community in India was able to manage this response with HOT support for mapper and validator recruitment, and documentation assistance.

Typhoon Molave landed in the Philippines in late October. Fortunately, the local community was actively mapping the area struck before the storm hit. Typhoon Molave quickly restrengthened and continued its course to Viet Nam, where HOT Indonesia has been working on the road network and the local community is starting to get organized. The Disaster Services Team worked with locals as well as the United Nations Development Program (UNDP) to identify the hardest hit areas, some of the final mapping of this response is still on the Tasking Manager (tasks.hotosm.org).

In the meantime, the Philippines was the target of two more storms - locally named Rolly and Ulysses. While things were busy globally, the HOT office in the Philippines as well as our global Disaster Services staff supported these responses with promotion of projects and documentation. Special thanks to Mapbeks and the local OSM Philippines group for the excellent management of these incidents. Some last remaining validation is required on the projects.

 CENTRAL AMERICA HURRICANES 


The first of two storms to strike Central America made landfall in Nicaragua. Hurricane Eta caused storm surge and flooding along the coast as well as major rain events to neighboring countries - Honduras, Guatemala, Panama and Colombia. Hurricane Iota, the latest recorded Category 5 storm in the Atlantic Hurricane season also targeted the Nicaragua coast just two weeks and 15 miles (9.3 kilometers) apart.

HOT has been managing the response in Nicaragua and Honduras as, although we were able to connect with local communities, they were unable to manage the request we had from Copernicus. There is still mapping left to do, visit the Tasking Manager to find the remaining projects, or visit the Hurricane Eta wiki-page for more information.

 AEGEAN EARTHQUAKE 

A 7.0 earthquake shook the Aegean Sea, causing major damage in Izmir, Turkey [6]. The local community was quick to respond and our partner in the region, http://yercizenler.org, jumped into action. HOT supported with mapper and validator recruiting, all while monitoring Typhoon Molave passing the Philippines and heading for it’s next landfall to Viet Nam.

 BEIRUT EXPLOSION 

A large amount of ammonium nitrate stored for years at the Beirut Port (Lebanon), exploded on August 4, 2020. The blast created serious damage in the city, and reports came in from as far as 24km from the explosion center. At that time, the young local community was enhancing its internal organization, the downtown area was already well mapped by locals. HOT led the mapping projects for an extended area beyond the city center to assist planning and service delivery of the Lebanese Red Cross. Visit https://openmaplebanon.org/ to learn more about the local communities efforts and what they plan to map next.

Other Collaborative Initiatives:

  • Ushahidi - Beirut Explosion is a platform designed for collecting needs and connecting resources.

  • Mapillary is being used to collect 'street-view' imagery of the damage, in turn used by agencies and organizations to estimate damage and coordinate relief efforts.

 COVID-19 RESPONSE 

In March 2020 the Disaster Services Team Activated to provide geospatial data, maps, and training to identified countries in need. Still ongoing, HOT’s global response to COVID-19 is the largest response ever undertaken by the organization. The gaps and needs of this response have originated and articulated an unprecedented level of internal coordination, between HOT’s different teams, and external collaboration with communities, organizations, agencies, and governments.

 LESSONS LEARNED 

2020 has been a convulsed year, but it has also brought the creation of a permanently-staffed global Disaster Services Team, and with it, the opportunity of deeper analysis on how HOT responds -or activates- to emergencies. Historically, it has been difficult to quantify the impact of responses, and 2020 has not been an exception. To tackle this challenge, the DST plans to ensure there is an After Action Review for a 100% of the activations.

Compliance with the OpenStreetMap Foundation’s Organized Editing Guidelines has been a challenge for partner organizations. Whether these are viewed as required or not, this past year there was at least one incident which possibly could have been avoided by following the guidelines.

Finally, where previously it was rare to find a country or even region that had an OSM community, now it is becoming rare that these do not have. It was great to observe many OSM communities strengthening, becoming self-sufficient, and able to lead disaster response locally. The latter has allowed HOT to switch its role, from leading to supporting these partners on its activations.
